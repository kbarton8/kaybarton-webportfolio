1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business.

Information technology (IT) is defined as "the application of computers and internet to store, retrieve, transmit, and manipulate data, or information" (Prasad, 2017:54) holds a huge purpose in modern business today for several reasons.
The majority of modern-day business have benefited hugely from the digital revolution even businesses you wouldn't image to have benefited have such as agriculture as farmers use computers for production planning, financial planning etc (Business Vibes, 2015).
IT has many obvious, simple purposes such as allowing businesses to sell products online, enabling communication, providing education however this is just the foundation.
IT allows business to:
- Access the tools needed to solve complex problems.
- Make better decisions
- Improve marketing
- Improved customer support
- Improved resource management
- Perform tasks quicker, providing efficiency

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects.

One legal guideline that is becoming increasingly important is the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The GDPR is the core of Europe’s digital privacy legislation (Palmer, 2019) and was adopted on April 14 2016 then enforced on May 25 2018. The purpose of GDPR is to allow EU citizens more control over their personal data and to simplify the regulatory environment for business so both citizens and businesses in the EU can fully benefit from the digital economy. GDPR is important as It improves the protection of European data subjects’ rights and clarifies what companies that process personal data must do to safeguard these rights (Visma, n/d). In the past, there have been countless breaches of data which can result in long lasting negative effects. One example of this is when the NHS suffered a data breach in 2018 which resulted in 150,000 patients in England being affected. Another recent case is the company EasyJet who are now facing an £18 billion class-action lawsuit over a data breach which resulted in the information of nine million customers being exposed (Osborne, 2020).
